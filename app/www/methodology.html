Methodology

The Nearest Neighbour Comparison Tool identifies GP Practices and Primary Care Networks (PCNs) with similar demographic, socioeconomic, clinical, workforce and financial characteristics.

The objective is to provide more meaningful benchmarking by comparing organisations operating in similar circumstances rather than relying solely on geographical proximity.

Dataset Construction

Two analytical datasets are produced:

  • Practice-level dataset
  • Primary Care Network (PCN)-level dataset

Individual source datasets are cleaned, transformed and integrated into a single record per organisation.

Population-weighted Characteristics

Several indicators are only available at Lower Layer Super Output Area (LSOA) level.

For these measures, the registered patient population of each practice is used to estimate the characteristics of the practice population.

Examples include:

  • Ethnicity
  • Indices of Deprivation
  • Underlying deprivation indicators
  • Population density

Demography

Age distributions are summarised using:

  • Population size
  • Lower quartile age (Q1)
  • Median age
  • Upper quartile age (Q3)
  • Interquartile range (IQR)

Statistics are calculated separately for males, females and the overall registered population.

Clinical Measures

Clinical indicators include Quality and Outcomes Framework (QOF) prevalence registers, achievement measures and personalised care adjustment rates.

Workforce Measures

Workforce indicators are derived from national General Practice and PCN workforce publications and include full-time equivalent staffing levels across major staff groups.

Financial Measures

Measures are derived from NHS Payments to General Practice and include:

  • Total payments
  • Mean payment per registered patient
  • Mean payment per weighted registered patient

Nearest Neighbour Identification

Before matching, all variables are standardised so that each measure has an equivalent contribution to similarity calculations.

Highly correlated variables are removed iteratively. Variables with an absolute correlation of 0.975 or greater are excluded until no highly correlated pairs remain.

This reduces duplication of information and prevents certain domains from having disproportionate influence on neighbour selection.

Distance Calculation

Each organisation is represented as a vector of standardised indicator values.

Similarity is measured using Euclidean distance between organisations. Smaller distances indicate greater similarity.

The distance matrix is calculated across all organisations and the ten closest organisations are retained as nearest neighbours.

Comparison Groups

  • National – nearest neighbours from England.
  • Same Region – nearest neighbours restricted to the same NHS England region.
  • Same ICB – nearest neighbours restricted to the same Integrated Care Board.

Important Notes

Nearest neighbours are not performance comparators.

They are organisations with similar characteristics and operating contexts. Differences in outcomes between similar organisations may help identify opportunities for quality improvement, service redesign, innovation and shared learning.
